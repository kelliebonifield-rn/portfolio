AI-Powered Clinical Coding Implementation

The Challenge

Manual code assignment across a 258-client medical coding operation was creating bottlenecks and limiting scalability. With an 8,890-person global delivery organization processing thousands of charts monthly, the company needed to dramatically improve coder productivity while maintaining regulatory compliance and accuracy standards across 49-state operations.

My Leadership Approach

1. User-Centered Discovery: Led comprehensive user research program interviewing 15+ clinical coders across US and offshore teams to identify workflow inefficiencies, adoption barriers, and productivity constraints. Synthesized findings into prioritized product requirements balancing AI capability with user acceptance.

2. Strategic Technology Selection: Evaluated Computer-Assisted Coding (CAC) vendors against criteria including EMR integration capability, explainable AI architecture, regulatory compliance, and scalability to support 258-client portfolio. Led cross-functional evaluation team (engineering, clinical, operations) through vendor demos, pilot assessments, and build-vs-buy analysis.

3. Risk-Mitigated Pilot Execution: Designed phased rollout strategy starting with 9-person pilot team processing 2,000+ charts monthly. Established success metrics, feedback loops, and go/no-go criteria. Coordinated 4 engineers and 5 clinical SMEs to integrate AI technology into existing workflow while maintaining zero service disruption.

4. Change Management at Scale: Created comprehensive training program, support infrastructure, and transparent communication strategy positioning AI as productivity enhancement rather than job replacement. Partnered with operational leaders across 3 countries to drive adoption across global coding workforce.

Results

  • ✓ 45% productivity improvement enabling team to scale capacity without proportional headcount growth
  • ✓ $480K+ annual savings ($120K quarterly) through workflow standardization and efficiency gains
  • ✓ 95% user satisfaction within 90 days, exceeding 80% adoption target
  • ✓ 99%+ coding accuracy maintained meeting CMS compliance standards across all clients
  • ✓ Zero service disruptionduring implementation across beta-client portfolio
  • ✓ Foundation for portfolio-wide AI strategynow expanding to RCM automation initiatives

Strategic Outcomes

This implementation became the cornerstone of the company's AI/ML transformation strategy, demonstrating ROI that justified further automation investments. The success model—emphasizing user trust, transparent AI, and phased adoption—was replicated across subsequent automation initiatives, contributing to the overall 45% efficiency improvement across the $30M+ Post-Acute portfolio.

Episode IQ Product Launch

The Challenge

A critical $1.3M client relationship was at risk due to inefficient episode management workflows across their 37-branch enterprise operation. Back office staff were manually reconciling episode data across multiple disconnected systems, creating 10+ hours of weekly administrative burden per branch and threatening client retention. The company had no internal SaaS capability—only service delivery—limiting scalability and valuation potential. Leadership needed a technology solution that could save the client relationship while demonstrating product development capability ahead of a strategic acquisition.

My Leadership Approach

1. Rapid Problem Validation & Business Case: Identified the at-risk client opportunity and built business case for the company's first internally-developed SaaS product. Conducted on-site discovery with client's back office teams across 3 pilot branches (8,000-admission annual volume) to quantify pain points, workflow inefficiencies, and ROI potential. Secured executive sponsorship and $300K development budget by demonstrating dual value: client retention + market expansion opportunity.

2. User-Centered Product Design: Led cross-functional product definition workshops with back office managers, clinicians, and administrators to identify highest-impact features. Created clickable prototypes and conducted 3 rounds of usability testing, iterating based on user feedback before committing engineering resources. Prioritized features using RICE framework (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) to ensure MVP would deliver immediate value while building foundation for expansion.

3. Agile Development Leadership: Partnered with 6-person engineering team to deliver MVP through 4-week sprint cycles with bi-weekly user feedback sessions. Coordinated with clinical SMEs to ensure regulatory compliance and integration with existing home health workflows.

4. Strategic Go-to-Market Execution: Developed comprehensive launch strategy including role-based training materials, video tutorials, in-app onboarding flows, and tiered support documentation. Created success metrics dashboard and feedback collection system. Positioned product as productivity enhancement rather than process disruption to accelerate adoption.

Business Impact

  • ✓ $1.3M client relationship rescued through rapid product delivery addressing critical pain point
  • ✓ 100% adoption across 3-branch pilot within 30 days, exceeding 80% target
  • ✓ 2 hours per user per week saved equivalent to 20% productivity gain for back office staff
  • ✓ Immediate enterprise expansion requested: Client demanded rollout to all 37 branches
  • ✓ Company's first internal SaaS product transitioned business model from services-only to scalable technology platform
  • ✓ Enhanced acquisition valuation: Demonstrated product development capability contributing to successful WellSky acquisition

Key Leadership Lessons

Episode IQ demonstrated that speed and rigor can coexist—delivering a market-ready SaaS product in 4 months by front-loading user research and ruthless feature prioritization eliminated costly pivots and drove 100% adoption. Strategic timing amplified impact: aligning the launch with a business-critical moment (at-risk $1.3M client + acquisition discussions) made Episode IQ proof of the company's evolution from services to scalable technology, directly contributing to acquisition valuation and demonstrating SaaS capability that unlocked future product investments.

Post-Acquisition Integration: RCM Platform Consolidation

The Challenge

Following WellSky's acquisition of Corridor in October 2023, the company needed to rapidly integrate two enterprise RCM platforms serving a combined 394 home health and hospice providers processing $30M+ in annual collections. The challenge: achieve aggressive cost synergies ($1M+ annual target) while maintaining 100% customer retention across a revenue-critical service during organizational transformation. Any service disruption risked client churn, regulatory compliance issues, and revenue loss across 50,000+ annual claims. Leadership set an ambitious 14-month timeline with zero tolerance for customer impact.

My Leadership Approach

1. Comprehensive Systems & Stakeholder Assessment: Led enterprise discovery mapping technology architecture, workflows, and data dependencies across both legacy platforms. Conducted stakeholder interviews with 25+ operational leaders, 15+ engineers, and 12 key clients to identify integration risks and change management requirements.

2. Strategic Roadmap & Sequencing: Developed phased 12-month integration roadmap balancing immediate cost synergies with long-term platform stability. Prioritized quick wins (vendor consolidation, duplicate system elimination) delivering early ROI while sequencing complex migrations to minimize risk. Defined clear milestones, success metrics, and go/no-go decision points with executive alignment.

3. Cross-Functional Integration Leadership: Coordinated 74-person team including 32 engineers, 27 clinical SMEs, 15 operational leaders, plus WellSky enterprise teams. Established weekly integration meetings, risk escalation protocols, and shared accountability framework managing competing priorities across product development, customer commitments, and integration deliverables.

4. Customer-Centric Risk Mitigation: Built comprehensive risk management framework with mitigation plans for 15+ potential customer impact scenarios. Established proactive communication cadence with all 394 clients including quarterly updates, dedicated integration support, and escalation paths. Created rollback procedures ensuring zero tolerance for billing disruption across 50,000+ annual claims.

Business Impact

  • ✓ $1M+ annual cost savings achieved ($250K+ quarterly synergies) through platform consolidation and vendor optimization
  • ✓ 2 months ahead of schedule: Completed 12-month integration in 10 months while maintaining quality standards
  • ✓ Zero service disruption: Maintained 99.9%+ uptime across 50,000+ annual claims during migration
  • ✓ Unified technology platform: Consolidated 2 legacy systems into single scalable architecture supporting future growth

Key Leadership Lessons

Successful M&A integrations require treating customers as the primary stakeholder, not an afterthought. By establishing proactive communication, building dedicated integration support, and maintaining zero tolerance for service disruption, we achieved 100% retention—a critical outcome given that 15-25% customer attrition is common in post-acquisition integrations. Cross-functional alignment across 74-person integration team required clear accountability frameworks, weekly synchronization, and executive sponsorship to resolve competing priorities between immediate cost synergies and long-term platform stability. Delivering 2 months ahead of schedule while exceeding cost targets ($1M+ annual savings) proved that realistic sequencing, early quick wins, and comprehensive risk management enable speed without sacrificing quality.
